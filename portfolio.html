Papa's Liquor & Deli

A neighborhood deli and liquor store's first website — built with online ordering and a real catering request tool.

Catering requests

Online ordering

The problem

No website, no online ordering, no catering pipeline

Papa's Liquor & Deli had no website at all — no way for customers to see the deli menu, check hours, order online, or ask about catering. Everything ran through walk-ins and phone calls, which meant catering inquiries in particular were easy to lose track of.

The approach

A catering form that replaces the voicemail

The centerpiece is a structured catering request form — event date, guest count, budget range, and menu preferences, all captured up front. Alongside it, the site pulls together deli, liquor, and third-party delivery ordering into one place, with live hours so customers always know if the store's open.

The result

Live, and already doing the job

The site is live and already handling what used to require a phone call. It's early — too soon to point to numbers — but Papa's now has an online presence that matches how seriously they run the business in person.

Hearth & Stone

A masonry and fireplace business, taking a referral-only reputation online for the first time.

Stonework built to last generations.

Custom fireplaces, patios, and retaining walls — see the finished work before you call.

Request a quote
The problem

A reputation capped by who already knew them

Hearth & Stone built a solid reputation the old way — word of mouth and referrals. But that meant growth was capped by their existing network, and anyone searching online had no way to find them or see the quality of a finished fireplace before calling.

The approach

Let the finished work do the convincing

The site is built around a project gallery — real finished builds front and center, organized by type of work — with a simple quote request form right alongside it, so starting a conversation takes one step, not phone tag.

The result

Referral-level trust, available to anyone

The concept shows how a trades business built on referrals can extend that same trust online, letting the work speak for itself without losing the craftsmanship-first feel that built the reputation in the first place.

The problem

Managing programs by memory and spreadsheet

Solo trainers and small gym owners were managing client programs the same way for years — spreadsheets, sticky notes, or memory. Updating a program mid-week or handing a client to another trainer meant starting from scratch.

The approach

Lead with the workout builder, not a dashboard

The hero leads straight into a live-feeling preview of the workout builder — picking exercises, setting sets and reps, assigning to a client — because that's the moment that actually convinces a trainer this saves them time.

The result

Sold through the workflow, not the pitch

The concept shows how a genuinely useful but unglamorous tool can be sold through the workflow itself, rather than generic dashboard screenshots or stock SaaS language.
